Time Management

Plan each day with simple steps so you can do more without stress.

Objectives

  • Learn to break the day into blocks and set priorities.
  • Use short, focused study sessions (20‚Äì30 minutes).
  • Practice estimating how long tasks take.

Try this

Make a simple daily plan: write three top priorities, then schedule two 25-minute focus sessions. Use a timer and check items off.

Key takeaways

Small blocks of focused work plus short breaks beat long, distracted study time.

Organization

Good organization helps you find things fast and reduces stress before class.

Objectives

  • Set up a simple folder system for school work (digital and paper).
  • Create a daily checklist for evening prep (pack, charge devices, check planner).
  • Keep a consistent place for important items (keys, planner, supplies).

Try this

Spend 10 minutes at the end of each day: sort papers, clear your desk, and pack your backpack for tomorrow.

Key takeaways

Small daily routines make mornings easier and help homework get done on time.

Goal Setting

Setting clear goals helps you focus and notice progress.

Objectives

  • Write one short-term and one medium-term goal.
  • Break goals into weekly steps you can do today.
  • Track progress and celebrate small wins.

Try this

Pick a goal for this week (e.g., finish a science project draft). Break it into 3 steps and schedule them across the week.

Key takeaways

Goals feel less scary when you break them into small, scheduled steps.

Study Habits

Good study habits help information stick and make homework faster.

Objectives

  • Learn how to plan focused study sessions with breaks.
  • Use active review (quiz yourself, explain aloud) instead of rereading only.
  • Create a simple study routine before tests.

Try this

Use the Pomodoro method: 25 minutes study + 5 minutes break. After four cycles, take a longer 20‚Äì30 minute break.

Key takeaways

Short focused sessions and active review beat long passive reading.

Digital Citizenship

Being safe and kind online helps you and others have better experiences.

Objectives

  • Understand privacy basics and strong password habits.
  • Practice respectful communication online.
  • Learn ways to limit screen time and keep balance.

Try this

Check privacy settings for one app or social site, and set a 30‚Äì60 minute daily screen-time limit for entertainment.

Key takeaways

Protect privacy, think before you post, and make rules that keep screens from taking over your day.
